Safety Planning

Creating a safety plan is an essential step in protecting yourself and your loved ones from intimate partner violence. A well-thought-out plan can help you respond effectively during a crisis and take proactive measures to ensure your safety.

Steps for Effective Safety Planning:

  1. Recognize the Warning Signs: Identify patterns of behavior that indicate escalating violence.
    2. Establish a Safe Space: Identify safe areas in your home where there are no weapons and where you can quickly escape.
  2. Have an Escape Plan: Plan how you will leave the house and where you will go. Memorize important phone numbers and keep essential documents and items accessible.
  3. Create a Support Network: Inform trusted friends, family, and neighbors about your situation. Establish a code word to signal them if you need immediate help.
  4. Prepare Your Children: Teach your children how to call 911 and establish a safe place for them to go during a violent incident.
  5. Document Everything: Keep a record of any incidents of violence, including dates, times, and descriptions. This documentation can be crucial for legal proceedings.
  6. Seek Professional Help: Contact local shelters, hotlines, and advocacy groups for additional support and resources.
